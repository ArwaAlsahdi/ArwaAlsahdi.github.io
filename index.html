About Me

I am a second-year PhD student with the GWUSEC Lab at The George Washington University, where I am advised by Prof. Adam Aviv. My research interests are in usable security and privacy. I use empirical and human-centered methods to study how the technical, legal, and organizational aspects of privacy come together in practice. My recent work has focused on two directions:

  1. Privacy Labels: Exploring app developers' practices around Apple's privacy nutrition labels, including why developers do (or do not) keep their labels up to date.
  2. Consent and Preferences: Studying how well consent banners align with users' actual privacy preferences.
